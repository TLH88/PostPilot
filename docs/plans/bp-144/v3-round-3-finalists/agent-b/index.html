Reading your draft
"…I had the data, I had the team's input, and I had a clear recommendation…"
Hook options
Your opening is strong but starts with setup. Try leading with the cost — leads with stakes perform better for leadership content.
"It cost us two months and one engineer. Here's why I let it happen."
"The hardest part wasn't being wrong. It was watching everyone agree with me."
Go deeper here
Para 2 ends with "we paid for it" but doesn't say how. Specificity here would triple the relatability. One number or one name is enough.
"…we paid for it — six weeks, one senior engineer who left, and the version of the product we never shipped."
Drafting closing options…
The question I ask now before every hard call is deceptively simple: "What would I do if I wasn't afraid of being wrong?" Not "what's the right answer" — that question is too easy to overthink. This one cuts through…
Closing options
End with the question (open loop — high engagement)
End with the lesson reframed as advice to your team
End with what you'd tell your past self
