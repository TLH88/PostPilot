Three years ago I made a call that cost us two months and one senior engineer. Last week the same situation came back — and this time I did it differently.

The first time around, I had all the information I needed. I had the data, I had the team's input, and I had a clear recommendation sitting in my inbox. What I didn't have was the courage to act on something that felt counterintuitive to the people above me.

So I deferred. I waited for consensus that never came. And we paid for it.

What changed the second time wasn't more information or more confidence. It was a single question I've started asking before every hard call: /ai closing
